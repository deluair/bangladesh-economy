Expanding the Export Base: Strategic Imperatives

Bangladesh's remarkable export growth has been predominantly driven by the Ready-Made Garments (RMG) sector, which accounts for approximately 83% of total export earnings ($46.99 billion in FY2022-23). This concentration presents a significant vulnerability in the country's export portfolio, particularly as Bangladesh prepares to graduate from Least Developed Country (LDC) status in 2026.

Export Concentration Metrics

  • Product Concentration: Bangladesh's export basket has a Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) of 0.73, indicating extremely high concentration compared to regional peers like Vietnam (0.31) and India (0.19).
  • Market Concentration: 58% of exports are destined for the European Union and the United States, creating vulnerability to economic slowdowns in these markets.
  • Value Chain Position: Approximately 75% of RMG exports are in the lower-value "cut, make, and trim" (CMT) segment, with limited participation in design, branding, or marketing.

Emerging Export Sectors

Several non-RMG sectors are showing promising export growth potential and warrant targeted policy support:

  • Pharmaceuticals: Exporting to over 150 countries with annual growth of 18.7% and a global market share approaching 0.2%. The sector benefits from the country's TRIPS waiver until 2033 and has established capabilities in generic formulations.
  • IT/ITES: Export earnings of $1.4 billion in FY2022-23 with a 25% CAGR over the past five years. Bangladesh has over 300,000 IT professionals and 4,500 software and IT service companies, with particular strengths in business process outsourcing and freelance services.
  • Light Engineering: Export value of $795 million in FY2022-23, primarily in bicycle manufacturing (second-largest exporter to the EU), agricultural machinery, and electronics components.
  • Agro-processing: Export earnings of $1.1 billion in FY2022-23, with processed food, vegetables, and fruits showing strong potential. Major exports include frozen shrimp, processed vegetables, and tea.
  • Leather and Footwear: Export value of $1.24 billion in FY2022-23, benefiting from shifting production from China. The newly established Savar Tannery Industrial Estate aims to improve environmental compliance and product quality.

Strategic Policy Framework for Export Diversification

The Export Policy 2021-2024 and the 8th Five Year Plan have prioritized export diversification through targeted interventions:

Key Policy Instruments

  1. Market Access Enhancement: Pursuing comprehensive economic partnership agreements with post-LDC graduation priority markets (Japan, India, China, UK) and streamlining Rules of Origin certification processes.
  2. Export Incentives Reform: Transitioning from direct cash subsidies (which may face WTO challenges post-graduation) to compliant support measures like innovation grants, quality certification assistance, and market development co-financing.
  3. Special Economic Zones: Developing sector-specific zones with customized infrastructure (e.g., active pharmaceutical ingredient parks, agro-processing zones) to address production constraints.
  4. Standards Compliance Support: Upgrading the Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution (BSTI) and sector-specific testing facilities to meet international standards and certification requirements.
  5. Export Financing: Expanding the capacity of the Export Development Fund from $7.5 billion to $10 billion and developing specialized financial products for emerging export sectors with distinct working capital requirements.

While the Ready-Made Garments sector remains the crown jewel of exports, Bangladesh's sustainable growth trajectory demands a diversified export basket that leverages the country's evolving comparative advantages in pharmaceuticals, IT services, light engineering, and agro-processing. Coordinated policy interventions across trade facilitation, industrial infrastructure, skills development, and standards compliance are essential to achieve this diversification imperative.
